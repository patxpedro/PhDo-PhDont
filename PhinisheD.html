So you are getting to the end or having made it through - firstly, CONGRATULATIONS!

So what next? Have a scroll for some of the options...

Data Scientist

That analytical mind isn't just for analysing your thesis data...

Data scientists utilise lots of transferrable skills from a science PhD. Data science is a multi-disciplinary field that uses scientific methods, processes, algorithms and systems to extract knowledge and insights from data in various forms, both structured and unstructured, similar to data mining.

Post-doctoral Researcher

In the unlikley event that you haven't had enough pipetting... try a post-doc!

A postdoc is a temporary position that allows a PhD to continue their training as a researcher and gain skills and experience that will prepare them for their academic career. Most postdoc positions are at a university or in industry, but there some postdocs positions at nonprofits and in government.

Medical Communications

Like scientific writing and communicating difficult scientific concepts to a lay audience?

Medical Communications describes a sector of the marketing mix which, in short, is anything that is not an advert or piece of media coverage. Medical communications range from publications to promotional medical education and is a strategic communication approach.

Medical Technology

Propelling science and medicine into the future...

The UK medical technology sector is a thriving ecosystem of researchers, scientists, engineers, designers and NHS clinicians working in an established industrial base. Together they drive the path of a novel technology from innovation, through the process of design and manufacture, and ultimately to the patient.

Pharmaceutical industry jobs

Drug development, clinical trials and marketing.

The pharmaceutical field offers a wealth of job and career opportunities for talented young graduates with a sound background in life sciences and various other academic disciplines. Jobs in the pharmaceutical indsutry use many of the same wet lab skills for early clinical testing but if you don't fancy going back into the lab, you can work in marketing, regulatory affairs and even HR.

Patent Law industry jobs

Putting that attention to detail to good use...

Patent law is the branch of intellectual property law that deals with new inventions. Traditional patents protect tangible scientific inventions, such as circuit boards, car engines, heating coils, or zippers. However, over time patents have been used to protect a broader variety of inventions such as coding algorithms, business practices, or genetically modified organisms.
